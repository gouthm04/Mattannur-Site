See & Do

Top Attractions & Activities

  • Pazhassi Dam & Garden — scenic picnic spot on the Mattannur–Iritty route; boating, amphitheatre, and historic charm. Perfect for early morning walks or evening photography by the water.
  • Palukachippara Viewpoint — mini-trek (7–10 km from town) with sunrise, airport, and hill views. Ideal for a quick nature escape or casual birdwatching.
  • Airport Spotting — café run + watch take-offs on clear evenings. Locals say the golden hour is unbeatable for photos.
  • Temple Theyyam Circuits — seasonal, dramatic, loud, unforgettable performances. Experience centuries-old rituals, vibrant costumes, and rhythm that will stay in your head.

Pro tip: combine a trek to Palukachippara with a sunset view over the hills, then stop at Pazhassi Dam for a peaceful night stroll.

“Mattannur town is very nearest to Kannur International Airport, yet surrounded by hills and heritage sites.” — District Portal

Local note: Many visitors underestimate how walkable and close these spots are — you can comfortably do two or three in a single day.

History & Heritage

Origins & Name

Mattannur (aka Mattannoor) literally means “the land of hills and rivers” in the local Malayalam dialect (mattu = hill, nūru = place/land). Its name reflects centuries of local language, trade routes, and hill-side settlements.

Pazhassi Raja

Known as the “Lion of Kerala,” Pazhassi Raja led daring resistance against the British East India Company in the late 18th century. His legacy is still felt in the hills and rivers around Mattannur.

Local Theyyam

Mattannur hosts unique Theyyam performances, with dramatic costumes, face paint, and centuries-old stories. Winter temple festivals are when the magic truly comes alive.

Colonial & Modern Era

From British-era roads to the modern Kannur International Airport, Mattannur has grown from a quiet hill town into a hub connecting heritage, hills, and contemporary energy.

Culture = Rhythm

The chenda is our unofficial town logo. Padma Shri Mattannur Sankarankutty Marar put Mattannur on the percussion map with thayambaka and panchari melam.

Festival season? Expect goosebumps, percussion walls, and happy chaos.

Getting Here

  • By Air: Land at Kannur International Airport (CNN) — it’s in Mattannur (≈3–5 km from town core).
  • By Road: Buses towards Mysuru/Bengaluru and to Kannur/Thalassery pass via town. Easy taxi/auto from airport.
  • By Rail: Alight at Kannur or Thalassery stations; connect by bus/taxi to Mattannur.
